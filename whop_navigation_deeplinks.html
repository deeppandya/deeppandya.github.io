Android now auto-verifies the paths added in this change (profiles, support, notifications, discover, and the store sections), so a tapped link opens the app. On the develop build, verification isn't automatic — enable it once in Settings → Apps → Whop (develop) → Open by default → Open supported links → add whop.com.

adb (always works, bypasses link verification). Pushes the URL as the deeplink extra:

Android-specific behavior notes

  • /@username and /u/username both resolve to the same native profile, ahead of bot-route parsing.
  • /discover and /notifications resolve to their top-level tabs before bot-route parsing, so they aren't mistaken for a company route.
  • Discover has no inner tabs on Android — every /discover/* opens the Discover overview.
  • Store sections (reviews / products / app-store) open the company/store page; scrolling to the specific section is a follow-up.
  • Single-segment store pages (e.g. whop.com/deep-ai-7c1d) are not auto-verified yet (Android manifest can't claim them without a catch-all) — they open via adb / in-app links only.
