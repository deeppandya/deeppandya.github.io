Three ways to run a raffle

Every raffle is different. Pick the model that fits what you're giving away.

Option 1 — One big prize

A single hero item — a graded card, a console, an e-bike. Sell a fixed number of tickets and draw one winner at the deadline. The classic drop.

Available in beta

Option 2 — Multi-winner bundle

Several prizes from one pool of tickets. Set a winner count and Whop draws them all at once, fairly, from the same verified entry list.

Available in beta

Option 3 — Digital & instant

Robux, gift cards, in-game items — no shipping. Winners are notified and delivered instantly the moment the draw closes.

How it works

Pick your prize

Choose a template or start blank, upload a photo, and our AI drafts a clean prize page in seconds.

Set price & tickets

Decide what a ticket costs and how many exist. Cap how many one person can buy to keep it fair.

Set the deadline

Pick when the raffle closes. Sales stop automatically — you don't have to babysit it.

Share one link

Drop your raffle link in chats, stories, and posts. Recruit affiliates to push it for a cut.

Draw & deliver

At the deadline we pick a winner fairly, you get paid, and shipping with tracking is built in.

A draw people can actually trust

The moment that makes or breaks a raffle is the draw. Whop picks the winner using verifiable randomness — every drawing comes with a published proof anyone can check. No "trust me," no off-camera spin. Buyers see the odds before they enter and the receipt after you draw.

draw result — winner + verifiable proof (seed · hash)
Every draw publishes a proof your audience can verify.

Get paid the moment it ends

Ticket revenue lands in your Whop balance as sales happen. Connect your bank once, and payouts run on the same rails creators already use across the platform. When you draw a winner for a physical prize, you collect their address and ship with tracking right from the dashboard.

One important note: raffles and sweepstakes are legally regulated, and the rules vary by region. During beta we're rolling out carefully and reviewing eligibility — check that raffles are available and permitted where you and your audience are before you launch.
